# "Hey IRIS" Wakeword - How It Was Trained


IRIS answers to a fully custom wake phrase, "Hey IRIS," detected on-device by
[openWakeWord](https://github.com/dscripka/openWakeWord) running on the Pi4 — no
cloud speech API, no Alexa/Google wake engine. Getting a custom wake word to a
reliable, low-false-positive state is a full small-scale machine learning training
run, not a config value:

- **Trained via** `briankelley/atlas-voice-training` — a Docker-packaged fork of the
  open-source openWakeWord training pipeline.
- **Positive samples:** 50,000 synthetic clips of the phrase "hey iris," generated by
  Piper TTS (`en-us-libritts-high`), covering a range of speeds/deliveries the TTS
  voice bank produces.
- **Negative/background data:** ~2,000 hours of pre-computed general-audio negative
  features (`ACAV100M` sample set, weighted 1024 per training batch — the dominant
  negative class) plus MUSAN music clips for background-noise augmentation, plus MIT
  room-impulse-response convolution for reverb realism, plus ~100 adversarial
  negatives per batch.
- **Training:** 100,000 steps, DNN classification head on top of the frozen Google
  embedding model, on an RTX 3090, ~6–8 wall-clock hours.
- **The human-relatable cost:** an RTX 3090 draws ~350W under sustained training
  load. 6–8 hours at that draw is roughly **2.5 kWh** — about the same energy as 25
  full MacBook Pro battery charges, running a home refrigerator for ~2.5 days, or
  leaving a 60W light bulb on for 40+ hours. One "yes, it heard me" moment costs a
  small but real amount of real-world electricity, and that's just for one
  training attempt.
- **Result (measured at threshold 0.50):** Recall 67.12%, Accuracy 83.45%,
  0.50 false-positives/hour.
- **It doesn't end at "trained."** An earlier attempt at this same wake phrase
  reportedly scored far better (97.1% recall, 0 FP) but was lost to a threshold
  misconfiguration and could not be recovered — the only copy was the deployed
  artifact, not a reproducible training recipe. A later attempt to broaden the
  positive set with real + synthetic voice clips actually made false-positives
  4x *worse*, not better. Every retrain is genuinely a fresh experiment, not a
  guaranteed improvement.

**Why the trigger threshold changed:** the confidence gate (`OWW_THRESHOLD`) started
at 0.50 and was raised to **0.70** after the deployed model began false-triggering
in the room on background conversation/noise — a real-world false-wake history, not
a lab-metric decision. Raising the threshold trades away sensitivity to quieter,
less-typical, or less-represented voices (see retrain plan below) in exchange for
not going off during normal household chatter.
